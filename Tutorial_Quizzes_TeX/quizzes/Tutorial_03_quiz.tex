\textbf{Time:} 10 minutes\\
\textbf{Format:} pen \& paper

\subsection*{Q1 (MLE + Fisher)}
Suppose $x_1,\dots,x_n$ are i.i.d. from $\mathcal N(\mu,\sigma^2)$ with known $\sigma$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item Derive the MLE for $\mu$.
\item Compute the Fisher information for $\mu$ and the Cram\'er--Rao bound.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Q2 (likelihood ratio)}
Consider two simple hypotheses $H_0$ and $H_1$ for a single observation with likelihoods $L_0(x)$ and $L_1(x)$. Define the likelihood ratio test statistic and explain how it is used to decide between $H_0$ and $H_1$.
